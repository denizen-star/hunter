## How It Works

### Phase 1: Preliminary Matching (Instant)
The system quickly identifies:
- **Exact Matches**: Skills that appear exactly in both your resume and the job description
- **Partial Matches**: Skills that are similar or related
- **Missing Skills**: Job requirements not found in your resume
- **Match Score**: Percentage of job requirements you meet

### Phase 2: Focused AI Analysis (1-3 seconds)
The AI then focuses on:
- **Experience Depth**: How well your experience matches the job requirements
- **Context Analysis**: Understanding the nuances of your qualifications
- **Recommendations**: Specific advice for improving your match

## Using the System

### 1. Submit a Job Application
1. Go to the **New Application** page
2. Paste the job description
3. Click **Analyze Qualifications**
4. Wait for the analysis to complete (1-3 seconds)

### 2. Review Your Results
1. **Check the Match Score**: This tells you how well you match the job
2. **Review Exact Matches**: These are your strongest qualifications
3. **Look at Unmatched Skills**: These are areas where you might need to improve
4. **Read Recommendations**: Follow the specific advice provided

### 3. Take Action
1. **For Strong Matches (70%+)**: You're well-qualified! Focus on interview preparation
2. **For Moderate Matches (40-70%)**: Consider addressing some gaps or emphasizing relevant experience
3. **For Weak Matches (<40%)**: This might not be the right fit, or you need significant skill development

## Common Questions

### Q: Why is my match score lower than I expected?
**A**: The new system calculates match scores based on job requirements, not your skills. This gives a more realistic view of how well you fit the job. A 60% match means you meet 60% of what the job requires.

### Q: What's the difference between exact matches and partial matches?
**A**: 
- **Exact Matches**: Skills that appear exactly the same in both your resume and the job description
- **Partial Matches**: Skills that are similar or related (e.g., "data analysis" matches "data analytics")

### Q: How can I improve my match score?
**A**: 
1. **Develop Missing Skills**: Work on skills that frequently appear in unmatched lists
2. **Update Your Resume**: Make sure all your relevant skills are clearly stated
3. **Gain Experience**: Look for opportunities to develop skills in areas where you're weak

### Q: What if I disagree with the analysis?
**A**: The system provides a foundation, but you know your experience best. Use the analysis as a starting point and adjust based on your knowledge of the role and your qualifications.

### Q: How often should I update my skills database?
**A**: Update your resume and skills whenever you:
- Learn new technologies or skills
- Complete new projects
- Get new certifications
- Change roles or responsibilities

## Troubleshooting

### Low Match Scores
**Problem**: Match score seems too low despite strong qualifications
**Solutions**:
1. Check if your resume includes all relevant skills
2. Make sure you're using industry-standard terminology
3. Consider if the job requirements are very specific or niche

### Missing Skills in Analysis
**Problem**: Important skills not appearing in the analysis
**Solutions**:
1. Check if skills are spelled correctly in your resume
2. Use variations of skill names (e.g., "Python" and "Python programming")
3. Make sure skills are clearly stated, not just implied

### Slow Analysis
**Problem**: Analysis taking longer than expected
**Solutions**:
1. Check your internet connection
2. Make sure Ollama is running properly
3. Try refreshing the page and submitting again
