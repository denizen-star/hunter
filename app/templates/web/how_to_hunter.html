Quick Start

Hunter streamlines your job hunting into three focused phases:

  1. Phase I: Application Process - Find jobs, analyze fit, and submit tailored applications
  2. Phase II: LinkedIn Networking - Research companies, find connections, and reach out to recruiters
  3. Phase III: Email Outreach - Find email addresses and craft personalized outreach emails

Each phase builds on the previous one, helping you stay organized and maximize your chances of landing interviews.

Phase I: Application Process

Goal: Find jobs, analyze fit, and submit tailored applications.

1Search LinkedIn

Log into LinkedIn.

2Filter Jobs

Search by area and filter by:

  • Remote/hybrid preferences
  • Pay range
  • Job title
  • Company

3Review Matches

Browse results that match your criteria.

4Capture Job Posting

4.1 Select the Job Text Navigate to the job posting (e.g., on LinkedIn). Click and drag your mouse to highlight all text starting from the Company Name all the way to the end of the Job Description.

4.2 Copy to Clipboard Copy the highlighted text to your clipboard using the keyboard shortcut Command + C (Mac) or Ctrl + C (Windows).

4.3 Open the HunterApp

4.4 Open "App Dash" dashboard on the left panel

4.5 Start a New Application In the top-right corner of the screen, click on the + New Application link.

4.6 Paste the Description Locate the Job Description * text box and paste your clipboard content using Command + V (Mac) or Ctrl + V (Windows).

5Extract Key Info & Analyze

The text box includes features to help you quickly populate the required fields. Follow these steps to organize the data:

5.1 Set Position Name Highlight the job title within your pasted text and copy/paste it into the Position Name field.

5.2 Set Company Name Identify the employer and copy/paste it into the Company Name field.

5.3 Insert the Job Link Scroll to the bottom of the job description box where HunterApp has organized a list of links. Find the correct Job Link and paste it into the corresponding field.

5.4 Run the Analysis Once the fields are populated, select the Analyze & Create Application button to finalize your entry.

6Start Application Form

Open the job posting link and begin filling out the company's form.

6.1 Resume Upload: Use "Connect with LinkedIn" if available; otherwise, upload an ATS-optimized resume (docx/PDF).

6.2 Job History: Reference Excel file for dates, descriptions, and skill highlights.

6.3 Cover Letter Placeholder: Continue form while Hunter finishes analysis.

7Review Match Score

Once the system processed your application you will see the Application Summary the summary will display the captured elements for:

7.1 Your Match Score in %

7.2 Company Name - Position

7.3 Location

7.4 Salary Range

7.5 Application Date

7.6 Last Updated (by job poster)

7.7 Job Posting Link

7.8 View Summary & Create Another buttons

8Review Analysis

8.1 Click the View Summary

8.2 Navigate the tabs -
    Summary,
    Raw Entry (with links at the bottom),
    Skills,
    Research,
    Qualifications,
    etc.

8.3 Also check the Networking tab to see if you already have any contacts at this company - this helps you identify existing relationships before reaching out.

9Update Status & Get Cover Letter

Go to Updates Tab

9.1 Select status to "Applied"

9.2 Write cover letter or select from Insert from template dropdown the bespoke cover letter created by Hunter. Learn more about templates ‚Üí

9.3 Modify and personalize the cover letter on screen

9.4 Copy the content of the textbox by clicking the copy button on the top right box

10Copy to Google Docs

Paste the cover letter text into a Google Docs template.

11Customize & Polish

Replace placeholders and remove redundant paragraphs.

12Save & Attach

Save as PDF and attach to the job submission.

13Document Submission

13.1 Copy the final cover letter back into Hunter's notes

13.2 Ensure status is "Applied."

13.3 Click "Update" button

14Verify Confirmation

Check email for receipts or verification requirements.

Phase II: Company Research & Networking

Goal: Understand the company, find connections, and reach out to recruiters.

1Research Company

Return to LinkedIn to search for the specific company.

2Check Company Page

Open the "People & Insights" tab.

3Analyze Company Data

Review employee count, geographic distribution, and hiring trends.

4Check Connections

Identify 1st-degree connections within the company.

5Check 2nd-Degree Connections

Look for mutual connections for potential introductions.

6Find Recruiters

Search for Recruiters, Talent Acquisition, or Hiring Managers.

7Gather Recruiter Information

Copy the recruiter's LinkedIn profile information: name, company, job title, profile URL, and profile details/text.

8Create Networking Contact in Hunter

In Hunter, go to "Network Dash" ‚Üí "New Contact" and create a networking contact with the recruiter's information.

8.1 Person Name: Enter the recruiter's full name (required)

8.2 Company Name: Enter their current company (required)

8.3 Job Title: Enter their position (optional)

8.4 LinkedIn URL: Paste their LinkedIn profile URL (optional)

8.5 Profile Details: Paste their resume, LinkedIn profile text, or articles (required)

8.6 Choose Processing Type: Select one of two options:

Full AI Processing - Complete analysis including match scoring, research, multiple message templates, and detailed insights (5-6 minutes)

Best for: Important contacts, hiring managers, or when you need comprehensive insights and all message templates

Simple Contact - Quick creation with basic personalized intro message only (30 seconds)

Best for: Recruiters, talent profiles, or when you just need a quick personalized intro message

9Review AI-Generated Messages

Open the networking contact's summary page and go to the "Messages" tab. Review the 4 AI-generated messages:

9.1 Initial connection request (<300 characters)

9.2 Meeting invitation

9.3 Thank you message

9.4 Consulting services offer

10Select & Copy Connection Message

Select and copy the initial connection request message (the first one, under 300 characters).

11Personalize & Send on LinkedIn

Paste into the LinkedIn message box, add the recipient's name and specific details, then send the connection request.

12Update Status in Hunter

After sending the LinkedIn connection request, go to the contact's "Updates & Notes" tab and update status to "Contacted - Sent".

13Add Notes

Add notes about the connection request (what you personalized, when you sent it) and save.

14View Contacts in Application Summary

All networking contacts are automatically linked to applications when their company name matches. To view contacts for a specific job:

14.1 Open the application's summary page from the dashboard

14.2 Click the "Networking" tab to see all contacts at that company

14.3 View contact details by clicking on any contact card to open their full summary in a popup overlay

14.4 Create new contacts directly from the application page using the "Create New Contact" button (pre-fills the company name)

Note: The Networking tab automatically shows all contacts with matching company names. No manual linking required! Use this tab to quickly see who you've connected with at each company and track your networking progress for each application.

Phase III: Email Outreach

Goal: Find email addresses and craft personalized outreach emails.

1Find Email Address

Use email finding tools to locate the recruiter's or hiring manager's email.

2Verify Email

Confirm the address is valid.

3Open Networking Contact in Hunter

Navigate to the networking contact's summary page for the recruiter/hiring manager you want to email.

4Review Research Tab

Go to the "Research" tab to review company insights, background information, and conversation topics that were AI-generated.

5Get Email Template

Go to the "Messages" tab for additional templates, or go to the main "Templates" page. Choose Recruiter Intro, Hiring Manager Intro, or Follow-up template. Learn more about templates ‚Üí

6Review Match Tab Insights

Check the "Match" tab for commonalities, differences, and suggested conversation topics to personalize your email.

7Customize Email Template

Replace placeholders with the recipient's name and add specific research insights from the Research and Match tabs.

8Copy Email Content

Copy the customized email text from Hunter.

9Paste to Email Client

Open Gmail/Outlook and paste the content.

10Final Personalization

Add a subject line and double-check recipient details.

11Attach Resume

Attach the tailored resume if suggested by the template.

12Send Email

Send the message.

13Update Networking Contact

In the networking contact's "Updates & Notes" tab:

13.1 Copy the sent email content into the notes

13.2 Add the email address with a timestamp

13.3 Update status (e.g., "Contacted - Replied" if they respond, or "In Conversation" if you're actively exchanging messages)

13.4 Click "Update" button

14Track Follow-up

Set a reminder to follow up in 5‚Äì7 days, or use the contact's Google Calendar integration button to schedule a follow-up reminder.
