"Mysteries" of the Hiring Process

Understand, how to meet specific ATS filters to come up on recruiters radar

January 5, 2026 • Hunter Team

1. Cracking the ATS Code

ATS are search engines that rank candidates based on keywords. Understanding how they work is crucial for getting your resume noticed by recruiters.

Best Practices for ATS:

The "Mirror" Technique:

Match job description terminology exactly. For example, if the job description says "Customer Success," use "Customer Success" in your resume, not "Client Relations." ATS do not understand synonyms. They look for exact keyword matches.

Standard Headers:

Use simple, standard headings like Work Experience, Education, and Skills. Creative titles like "My Professional Journey" can confuse the software and cause your resume to be parsed incorrectly.

Avoid "Fancy" Formatting:

Many ATS cannot "read" text inside tables, text boxes, or graphics. This means parts of your resume might appear blank on the recruiter's screen. Stick to simple, clean formatting with standard text blocks.

The 80% Rule:

You don't need a 100% keyword match. Aiming for 80% is sufficient. The system highlights candidates who meet a certain threshold, so focus on matching the most important keywords rather than trying to match every single word.

2. The "Direct Message" Formula

Give recruiters or company peers on LinkedIn a compelling reason to respond. Beyond just stating "Hi, I applied," you need to provide value and make a specific ask.

Structure of a winning message:

  1. The Hook: Mention the specific role and confirm that you've already applied.
  2. The Value: Include one sentence about a significant achievement (e.g., "I recently led a team that cut costs by 15%").
  3. The Ask: Request a specific, low-friction next step (e.g., "Who would be the best person to speak with regarding this role?").

Example Message:

Hi [Name], I recently applied for the [Title] role and noticed it was just reposted. Given my background in [Specific Skill] and my success in [Achievement], I'm confident I could hit the ground running. I've attached my resume here just in case—would you be open to a brief chat or pointing me toward the right hiring lead?

3. The "Hidden" Interview: Networking

Networking is often the most effective way to get noticed, but it requires a strategic approach. Many positions are filled through referrals before they're even posted publicly.

Building genuine relationships within your industry can open doors that applications alone cannot. Focus on providing value in your interactions, whether that's sharing insights, offering help, or connecting people who could benefit from knowing each other.
